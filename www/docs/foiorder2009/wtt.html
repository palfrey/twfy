To read more background to mysociety’s position, read this Blog post. Suggestions for some things you could say:

  • Ask them to vote against the draft Freedom of Information (Parliament) Order 2009 this Thursday. (The Order and Explanatory Memorandum (PDF))
  • Ask them to sign Jo Swinson’s Early Day Motion (details on Jo Swinson’s site)
  • Don’t assume your MP is against releasing expenses information –
    • This measure was introduced quietly at the last minute;
    • Your message may be the first they’ve heard of it;
    • Many MPs are strongly in favour of greater transparency;
    • Above all, and as ever, be polite.
  • Let them know that you want Parliament to be more open, not less, and that this is the wrong direction of travel.
  • Tell them that TheyWorkForYou will prominently display whether or not they vote for or against this order, and that both voting for and abstention will be noted as a vote against greater transparency.
  • Ask them whether they’d think it was acceptable if other public servants didn’t have to account for their expenses.
